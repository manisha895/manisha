Interests:

Dachshunds, winemaking - and computers!

It wasn't always that way, though. Back in 1989 when my then-husband brought home our first desktop (a huge old 386 with a bulky CRT monitor) I said I would never use it. Never ever. Give me a small electric typewriter, and I'd be pleased.

This is a proclamation that I'm pleased to say didn't last. It didn't take me long to morph into first a tentative user, then an avid one. Websites for the local gymnastics club, the provincial rhythmic gymnastics association, and other volunteer organizations followed.

The advent of computers as allowed me the freedom to do quite a bit of photography - you can find samples in the Personal Artwork section - weddings, family photos, travel photos, and editorial.

And of course, the digital age facilitated my writing - a hobby which turned into a profession when I was hired at a regional newspaper (and just over a year later, became editor there). When the Adobe Suite of design products were introduced at the newspaper, I became keenly interested in that; enough to start my own company, where I did corporate branding, policy writing, ad design, freelance writing, basic IT services and safety record keeping. Computers - once so despised - brought me to the world of communications; and it brought the world to me.

In 2010 I joined the Corporate Communications world at SUMA, and then in 2012 at the Saskatchewan Chamber of Commerce, both of which honed my communications skills in a number of areas.

I am a firm believer in lifelong learning. If you're not learning, you may as well be dead. I successfully pursued my Certified Associate in Project Management (CAPM) in 2015, followed by the New Media Communications Certificate at Saskatchewan Polytechnic in 2016-17. In addition, I have completed numerous application-based certificates over the years to round out my digital abilities.

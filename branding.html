Logo Exercises

Some examples of branding exercises from the New Media Communications program. St. Marguerite Bourgeoys School was seeking a new logo, mascot and newsletter format.

Date: Winter 2017 Client: St. Marguerite Bourgeoys School, Regina

The Target

The school was looking for a logo which fit with the existing school colours (blue and yellow) and which reflected the school's rich history. I incorporated the Regina Catholic School Board logo with a stylized shield, a book for learning and a modern font.

Our Task

The task in this instance was to research the school's provenance, its current colour palette, and come up with a consistent, attractive and modern branding scheme. Letterhead, envelope, business cards and a school newsletter template were all part of the project.

The Skills

This project required skills in graphic design, a knowledge of iconography, research techniques, and proficiency with the Adobe suite of products, including Photoshop CC and InDesign.

The Result

While the school ultimately decided to go in a different direction, I believe this logo suite is a good representation of its heritage and its mission.

ChamberGeek

This smaller project involved developing a brand, website and presentation for the 2016 Saskatchewan Chamber of Commerce Conference on Business. The website may be seen on the Web Projects page, or at http://www.chambergeek.net

Date: May 2016 Client: Saskatchewan Chamber of Commerce

The Target

The Chambergeek logo was designed to be a "fun" project, to accompany a website for a presentation to managers of local Saskatchewan Chambers of Commerce. The logo was meant to reflect my own interest in IT and computer tools available to nonprofit organizations, as well as to celebrate "women geeks".

Our Task

The task was to compile a list of tools for chamber executives and other nonprofit workers. The logo is a caricature representation of a brunette "geek" with a stylized typeface.

The Skills

This project utilized my skills in Adobe Photoshop and website CMS. I found a stylized font to enhance the logo.

The Result

The result was a successful presentation at the Conference, and a lasting collection of valuable tools for nonprofits in the province.

Carson Energy Services Rebranding

While dated now, this project displays the range and depth of my branding services. Prior to being bought by Flint and URS, Carson Energy Services was the largest privately held energy services company in southeast Saskatchewan. I was pleased to perform a complete corporate rebranding for the company.

Date: September 2011 Client: Carson Energy Services Ltd.

The Target

The client wanted to retain the "Carson" part of the logo, but wanted the remaining wording added. After an unsuccessful search for a suitable font, I edited an existing font for a unique look. The entire project took over a year. Components included designing the look for the website, photography, stationery and cards, presentation templates, and a full handout package.

Our Task

Beginning with the logo and working up from there, every aspect of the 14-location business required rebranding. Management wanted to retain the legacy "Carson" part of the logo, which was created when the business had only two locations and a limited range of services. The logo had to do that, while reflecting the company's growth and diversity of services. Working with a web coder, I designed the website and marketing collaterals, as well as stationery, business cards, signage and presentation templates, and created a comprehensive corporate identity manual.

The Skills

During this project, I was able to employ my skill in design as well as expertise in:

  • Adobe Photoshop
  • Adobe InDesign
  • Microsoft PowerPoint
  • Adobe Acrobat
  • Font Forge
Knowledge of current trends in marketing, background in the oilfield services industry, and the basics of design and typography.

The Result

The result was a complete overhaul of a dated logo to reflect the company's increased size and capacity. Within a short time, CES was on the market, and the collaterals were used as part of the sales pitch to Flint Energy Services. The company continued to use the logo (with an addition of "a Flint Company" until it was sold and merged with URS.
